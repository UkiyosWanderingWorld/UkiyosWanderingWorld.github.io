Another Day, Another Revelation

2025年3月10日

Last night I had many dreams (nothing out of the usual there) the majority of which I couldn't remember in the slightest. The usual sleep routine continues to plague my existence - adding a slight bit more depth to my already deeply engraved eye shadows (At this rate we'll reach the mantle of my bones in a few weeks haha).

Yes, the same ol' same ol'.

In recalling this now, how could I give voice to the rush of emotions welling up inside me just a few hours prior - all the while the quickly stagnating silence being punctuated by the chicken-like squabblings of the last remaining coworker in the 教員室, seemingly tortured by the endless stream of unexpected surprises that come his way in the tizzying day-to-day operation of a prefectural high school.

Yes, now it is just me and the quiet librarian taking notes on something I couldn't have the slightest idea of, the importance of which I have to wonder will forever evade me... and with that, she is gone in a flash through the 引きドア (I learned from a friend btw that this door is not a pull-type door, as you might expect, but rather a sliding-type).

In this way, while thinking about things that have already escaped me, I am surrounded by a world that refuses to stop moving. I suppose inertia is that sort of thing in the first place. More than that I consider whether reflection is even worth my while. Whatever sequence of neuron firings that (I) somehow will to be through (volition?), an afterimage of the world as it was, to this creature called by myself as "me," only a semblance of the reality that was then can be reconstructed. Even now, as I sit in quiet contemplation of these, if I may be honest, bizarre circumstances, I am constructing not the original as I have intended, but something altogether new, informed by the present moment - feeling, mood. While many would like to believe they act in complete accordance with their own desires - an individual of individuals, you might say - we are all still privy to the environment in which we are embedded. Even more than this are the physiological constraints that we operate within. Even now, my blood sugar level slowly wanes, the caffeine I have consumed has already worn off due to the adenosine receptors having already upregulated to prepare for my daily dose at the same time of day. Like clockwork, what I felt in midst of a torrent of physiological changes I cannot accurately describe in my rather well-equilibriated present condition.

At the same time, however, in the midst of this quiet thought that I may call my very own, with this reflection I can take a moment to appreciate the change that has inevitably taken over me over say these past ten years as I soon enter the 30th year of my still young life. In those days when I came across such a fact of life I would've been prone to silent melancholy. If I may reinterpret those emotions in my present mind, such a feeling of 「しかたがない」was more defined by debilitating defeatism than it is now by blissful acceptance. This current me now sees this as an unescapable fact of life rather than an inevitable fate which prevents me from reaching that aspired world of my dreams. To the me of now I consider such an accurate reconstruction of the past to be the same type of process as that of reconstructing those dreams. Present circumstances dictate what we are even able to recall of the past, near or however distant. Whatever mode of operation we enter into in the midst of our waking hours, from the intense focus I feel when writing this post now, to the constantly irritated, scatterbrained, and utterly disturbed state of mind I experienced yesterday afternoon. In one such way each state of mind is informed by physiological processes that we can control to perhaps only an unsatisfactory degree.

Shift. 休憩. My mind was getting carried away with a single thought and a change was needed, a rest. while in the urinal, overlooking the same carbon-based, now long-past being effective in the least air-freshener as always, I recall the words of one of the prefectural advisors at the Okayama JET orientation last Fall. 「挑戦」. The germ of the idea behind that simple utterance was precisely what the term itself implies: challenge. In the next moment, another word flashed through my mind - 「勇気」. The task that I had been ignoring for literal weeks suddenly I felt I just had to challenge myself to. The same emotions welling up as always once I dialed the number, the deliberate preparation of materials beforehand to allieve the me of the moment from being at a loss of words - a developed trauma which has only deepened with time. Despite all of these thoughts and feelings swirling inside me like a maelstrom, one 諺 came to mind: 「聞くは一時の恥、聞かぬは一生の恥」. In short, "To ask brings with it a moment of embarrassment, but not to ask is as a lifetime of embarrassment." This phrase, spinning within my head like a dredel released from juvenile hands, acted as the final push to call. A simple phone call. One of many that occur each and every day. In my work I hear endless phones crying for attention - as an essential tool for information transmission, a tool invented several hundred years prior, past the need for its advancement in the mid 2000s, all the while still used varying due to custom and culture. Something as simple as this, I avoid like the plague.

Challenge. What really pushed me beyond the verge of no return? The final moment which separated decision from indecision in the endless complex of neurons, muscle, proteins and tissues which constitute me, I am as yet unlearned enough to give a definitive answer. But in those critical moments, another thought, well, less a thought and more a feeling informed by volition of days past: "I want to live here for the rest of my life." Something as trivial as a phone call cannot stop me from achieving this dream. In a society which still operates heavily under the influence of the communication method of the humble phone call, I have no other choice but to challenge myself to uncondition myself from this fear. Even now I don't think my mind and body are in alignment with processing the call itself.

Yet after all this is said, the most incredible thing happened - nothing at all. In moments when I did not understand my phone partner the first time, I employed my language skills to ask again. Despite not understanding even after offering a response to the second time it was stated, through a bit more communication, an agreement was achieved, a time set, hairstylist determined. In short - mission accomplished. In shorter short: I learned essential phrases that employees use in such exchanges.

As I move through my day, as my body continues to revolve around this concept of "homeostasis," as I once again forget my water bottle and feel the effects of slight dehydration, I can at least sit for a quiet moment in a feeling of accomplishment. One obstacle in the path. One challenge during the climb. For a moment, I can wrap all of my fears, my emotions warped from times past together with my dreams, the things that I aspire for whenever in the future, into a delicious meal that I may call "peace."

この本から学んだことを今後のプロジェクトに活かしていきたいと思います。
